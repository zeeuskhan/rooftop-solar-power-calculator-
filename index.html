Solar system size guide for Residential Homes

Choosing the right system size is critical to maximizing your Return on Investment (ROI). While a 6.6 kW system is the most common install in Australia due to inverter limits and STC rebate levels, your needs may vary based on household size and appliance usage.

Frequently asked questions

How many solar panels do I need for my home in Australia?
The number of panels depends on your daily electricity usage and location. A typical Australian home using 20 kWh per day needs approximately 16–20 panels (400W each) for a 6–8 kW system. Use our calculator above to get a personalised estimate.
How much does rooftop solar cost in Australia in 2025?
In 2025, a 6.6 kW solar system in Australia costs between $5,000 and $8,000 after the federal STC rebate. Prices vary by state and installer. Our calculator uses current average pricing to estimate your upfront cost.
What is the payback period for solar panels in Australia?
Most Australian homeowners see a solar payback period of 3 to 7 years, depending on system size, electricity usage, feed-in tariff rate, and how much solar energy is self-consumed. Sunnier states like Queensland and Western Australia typically have shorter payback periods.
What feed-in tariff rates apply in each Australian state?
Feed-in tariff rates in 2025 vary by state: NSW ~5–7c/kWh, VIC ~4–6c/kWh, QLD ~5–8c/kWh, SA ~5–7c/kWh, WA ~7–10c/kWh. These are minimum regulated rates; some retailers offer higher rates.
What is the PM Rooftop Solar Yojana (PM-Surya Ghar)?
The PM Rooftop Solar Yojana, now known as PM-Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana, is a government scheme targeted at residential households. It provides substantial subsidies for installing solar rooftop systems (up to 3kW). Our pm rooftop solar calculator helps you estimate the system size needed to benefit from this solar rooftop gov in initiative.

Many major financial institutions offer specialized green energy loans. Our solar rooftop loan calculator sbi and boi solar rooftop loan calculator presets use common interest rates ranging from 8.5% to 10.5%. How do I calculate solar panel output in Australia?
Solar panel output = panel wattage × peak sun hours per day × system efficiency. Australian cities receive 4 to 6 peak sun hours per day on average. A 400W panel in Sydney (4.5 peak sun hours) at 80% efficiency produces approximately 1.44 kWh per day.

Tips to maximise your solar savings

  • Optimise Self-Consumption: Run major appliances (dishwashers, washing machines, pools) during the middle of the day.
  • Consider North Orientation: While East/West orientations are good for morning/evening usage, North-facing panels produce the most total energy.
  • Regular Maintenance: Keep panels clean and ensure no trees are shading your system as it grows over the years.
  • Monitor Performance: Use an app to track your generation and catch any inverter faults early.
